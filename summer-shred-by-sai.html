5-Day Push · Pull · Legs · Upper · Lower
Compound-First Training Split
💡
How this is built: Every day opens with a heavy compound lift — squat, deadlift, bench, overhead press, barbell row. These are the movements that build the most muscle and burn the most calories. Isolation work follows to target specific muscle groups for shape and separation. This is the same structure as an advanced split, scaled to your current capacity.
Day 1
Push — Chest · Shoulders · Triceps
Compound strength + isolation detail
Push
Chest — Primary
  • Barbell Bench Press compound Primary compound — maximum chest + shoulder + tricep stimulus
    4 × 5–8
  • Incline DB Press compound Upper chest — the shelf that shows when lean. Slow 3s eccentric.
    3 × 10–12
  • Cable Fly — Low to High isolation Serratus + outer pec sweep. Peak squeeze at centre.
    3 × 12–15
Shoulders
  • Seated DB Shoulder Press compound Full overhead range — overall shoulder mass builder
    3 × 10–12
  • DB Lateral Raise isolation Side delt — makes shoulders look wide and capped
    4 × 15–20
  • DB Front Raise isolation Anterior delt — creates the front delt / bicep separation groove
    3 × 12–15
Triceps
  • Overhead Tricep Extension — DB or Cable Long head stretch — largest tricep head, upper arm thickness
    3 × 12–15
  • Rope Pushdown isolation Lateral head — horseshoe detail. Elbows pinned to sides.
    3 × 15

📈
Progressive overload — the only rule that grows muscle: Every week, aim for either 1 more rep than last week, or 0.5–1 kg heavier on the bar. Write it down in a notebook or your phone after every session. If you are lifting the same weight for the same reps week after week — you are not building muscle, you are maintaining. Track it.

Why Each Day Is Structured This Way

  • Compound first, isolation after: Heavy compound lifts (bench, squat, deadlift, row, OHP) require full energy and CNS output. Always hit these first when you're fresh. Isolation exercises after compound work is pre-fatigued and produces more targeted stimulation.
  • Traps trained on Pull day + Upper day: Shrugs on Day 2, farmer's carries or shrugs optional on Day 5. Deadlifts on Day 6 hit mid-trap heavily. Traps need volume across the week — they recover fast.
  • Biceps hit twice — short and long head: Day 2 = barbell curl (short head) + incline curl (long head) + hammer. Day 5 = preacher curl (short head peak) + spider curl. Both functions covered both weeks.
  • Cardio = 3 sessions of 20 min max: Enough to accelerate fat loss without eating muscle. Diet does the real work — cardio is just a multiplier. More is not better for a recomp goal.

🍽️
The approach: South Indian food is naturally excellent — the problem is the rice-to-protein ratio. Most South Indian plates are 70% rice and 30% everything else. We flip that. Same dishes, same flavours — restructured portions. One strategic rice serving near your workout. Everything else high protein, low carb.

South Indian Food Guide
What's in. What's out.
✓   Eat freely — your daily staples
  • 🐔Chicken breast, pepper chicken varuval, chicken kuzhambu (less oil)
  • 🐟Seer fish, rohu, tilapia, sardines, anchovies — grilled or tomato curry
  • 🥚Eggs — muttai kuzhambu, scrambled, boiled, any style
  • 🫘Dal, sambar, rasam, kootu — all lentil-based dishes freely
  • 🥦All vegetables — brinjal, drumstick, beans, cauliflower, spinach, capsicum
  • 🧆Paneer — in portions, excellent protein source
  • 🥛Curd (thayir), buttermilk (mor) — no added sugar
  • 🥜Roasted peanuts, chana — small amounts as snacks only
  • ☕Black coffee, green tea, plain black tea — no sugar
  • 💧Water — 3–3.5 litres daily. Non-negotiable in Tamil Nadu heat.
✗   Reduce or eliminate
  • 🍚Large rice portions — 1 small cup near workout only, nothing more
  • 🫓Idli, dosa, upma in excess — high glycemic, avoid as main meals
  • 🥣Idiyappam, pongal, puttu, appam — high carb, low protein breakfast
  • 🍬Payasam, halwa, jalebi, ladoo, murukku — sugar + fat combination
  • 🧃Sugary drinks, packaged juices, sweetened chai, Horlicks with sugar
  • 🫕Coconut milk-heavy gravies — replace with tomato-based versions
  • 🍟Bajji, bonda, vada — deep fried + refined flour
  • 🛢️Excess oil in cooking — 1 tsp per meal is enough
  • 🍪Biscuits, bread, white flour snacks between meals
  • 🥛Sweetened chai multiple times a day — switch to black tea or coffee

The 3 Rules That Drive the Whole Plan

  • Hit 155g protein every day — no exceptions. This single number determines whether your body builds muscle or just loses weight. Undereating protein during a cut means the weight you lose is partly muscle. That makes the skinny fat look worse, not better. Protein first, every meal.
  • One rice serving per day, timed near your workout. Not zero carbs — just strategic carbs. Your muscles need glycogen to train hard. Have your rice before or after training. Remove it from every other meal and replace with more protein and vegetables.
  • South Indian food is already excellent — just restructure the ratios. Sambar, fish curry, kootu, pepper chicken, rasam — all of these are high quality foods. The problem is the proportion of rice to everything else. Flip the ratio: large portion of protein-based dish, small portion of rice or none.
📊
Track for 3 weeks: Use MyFitnessPal or a simple notebook. Three weeks of tracking trains your eye permanently. You won't need to track forever — just long enough to understand your portions.
